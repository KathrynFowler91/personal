About

Kathryn Fowler is an atmospheric science PhD student at The University of Manchester. An interest in using her mathematical ability to solve real world problems has lead her to study aerosol cloud interactions using computer models.

Most recently Kathryn has investigated the composition of aerosol particles using diffusion frameworks, her results can be found in the article; Maxwell-Stefan diffusion: a framework for predicting condensed phase diffusion and phase separation in atmospheric aerosol.

Kathryn graduated from The University of Manchester with a First Class Masters Degree in Mathematics and Physics, combining her undergraduate studies with an athletic career, representing the GB Women's Water Polo team at international competitions, culminating in becoming a Commonwealth Champion in Aberdeen, 2014. She continues to play water polo at a national level and coaches the University team. In her spare time, she really enjoys writing about herself in third person.
